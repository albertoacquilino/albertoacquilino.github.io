Teaching Experience

Selected roles and courses taught in higher education and community programs.

  • 2025

    Guest Lecturer — McGill University, Montreal, Canada

    AI in Music Education

    Exploring artificial intelligence and its applications in music education, integrating core concepts in AI, machine learning, and generative systems with practical musical examples. The course examines how AI models are trained, their possibilities and limitations, and includes a research-informed case study on the automatic assessment of sound production efficiency in instrumental performance, as well as algorithmic and generative AI-based composition. Pedagogical, ethical, and inclusive perspectives are emphasized, positioning AI as a supportive tool for creativity, assessment, and contemporary music teaching practice.

  • 2024 – Present

    Lecturer & Curator — Global Leaders Institute, Global Arts MBA

    Course: Emerging Technologies for Arts Innovation

    The course examines how cutting-edge technologies are transforming the arts. Participants explore innovative approaches to audience engagement, artistic creation, and large-scale immersive experiences, gaining insights into the evolving landscape of art and technology.

  • 2023 – 2025

    Lecturer — McGill University, Montreal, Canada

    Music Technology Course MUMT 202: Fundamentals of New Media

    A theoretical and practical introduction to selected areas of music technology. Topics include digital audio and sampling theory, MIDI and sequencing, audio editing and mixing, sound recording, score editing software, and current areas of research interest.

  • 2023 – 2024

    Guest Lecturer — McGill University, Montreal, Canada

    Focused classes on modern wind instruments pedagogy, combining fundamental performance techniques (e.g., breathing, warm-ups, embouchure and sound production) with innovative teaching strategies and classroom technologies. Through practical, hands-on sessions, students gain contemporary tools for effective instruction and musical performance.

  • 2022 – 2023

    Teaching Assistant — McGill University, Montreal, Canada

    Music Technology Course MUMT 203: Introduction to Digital Audio

    An introduction to digital audio and the technologies involved in its practical realization and use in computer music. Topics include audio signals and systems, sampling and quantization, signal encoding, compression, transmission and storage, filters, A/D and D/A conversion, digital audio effects, sound sampling, and synthesis techniques.

  • 2022

    Invited Teaching Artist — Sistema New Brunswick (Moncton & Saint John), Canada

    Music performance and ensemble direction focused on youth music education

    Sistema New Brunswick is a free, intensive after-school orchestral music program for children and youth that uses ensemble music-making as a vehicle for social development. Inspired by the global El Sistema movement, the program emphasizes inclusion, teamwork, and commitment, helping young people build confidence, a sense of belonging, and positive connections within their communities through collective music education.

  • 2014 – 2016

    Music Performance Teacher — Pro Musica Association, Italy

    Individual and group brass instruction

    A community-based, non-profit cultural organization dedicated to the promotion of music, arts, and local heritage through inclusive education and cultural initiatives. Rooted in its local territory, it fosters social cohesion, intergenerational exchange, and personal development by offering music education, creative workshops, summer programs, and public events.

  • 2015

    Music Teacher — Maria Ausiliatrice Middle School, Genoa, Italy

    Music Education Course

    An introduction to music literacy and performance through an integrated study of notation, rhythm, melody, and harmony. Topics include vocal practice, basic instrumental technique, ensemble playing, active listening, and an overview of musical styles and traditions, with an emphasis on collaboration, creativity, and expressive skills.

  • 2013 – 2014

    Music Performance Teacher — Arenzano Philarmonic, Arenzano, Italy

    Ensemble coaching and instrumental instruction

    A community-based musical association dedicated to preserving and renewing the local music tradition through performance, education, and cultural engagement. Deeply rooted in its territory, it promotes music as a shared social practice by offering band-focused music training, a youth ensemble, and public concerts, while providing instruments and learning opportunities to young musicians through collaborations with schools, public institutions, choirs, orchestras, and international artists.
